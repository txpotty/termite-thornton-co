Comprehensive Termite Control Services

Our comprehensive termite control services are designed to address every stage of a termite infestation, from initial detection to long-term prevention. We understand that every property is unique, and our treatment plans are tailored to meet your specific needs and the extent of the infestation.

Thorough Termite Inspections

Identifying the presence and scope of a termite infestation is the critical first step. Our inspections are meticulous and thorough, going beyond the obvious signs. We utilize advanced tools and techniques to detect hidden termite activity, even in challenging-to-reach areas.

Exterior Inspections

The exterior of your home is often where subterranean termites, the most common type in Colorado, gain entry. We carefully examine the foundation, siding, decks, porches, and any wood-to-soil contact points. We look for mud tubes, damaged wood, and any other evidence of termite foraging activity. Trees, stumps, and wood piles near your home are also inspected as they can be attracting environments for termites.

Interior Inspections

Inside your property, we inspect basements, crawl spaces, attics, and all accessible wooden structures. We check for damaged drywall, bubbling paint, warped wood, and any signs of termite droppings or wings. Particular attention is paid to areas around windows, doors, and plumbing fixtures, as these are often entry points for termites.

Our experienced inspectors are trained to recognize the subtle nuances of termite activity, ensuring that no potential infestation goes unnoticed. A comprehensive inspection provides the foundation for an effective treatment strategy.

Targeted Termite Treatment Plans

Based on the findings of our inspection, we develop a customized treatment plan. We explain our recommended approach, the products we will use, and what you can expect throughout the process. Our goal is to not only eliminate the active infestation but also to establish protective barriers to prevent future issues.

Liquid Termite Treatments

Liquid termiticides are a highly effective method for creating a protective barrier around the perimeter of your property. These treatments are applied to the soil around the foundation, preventing subterranean termites from entering the structure. We use professional-grade products that are designed for long-lasting residual effects, providing continuous protection.

The process typically involves trenching or rodding around the foundation, ensuring the termiticide is dispersed properly to create a complete barrier. This method is particularly effective in preventing termites from accessing the wood in your home.

Termite Baiting Systems

Termite baiting systems are another effective control method, particularly for monitoring and eliminating termite colonies. These systems involve installing bait stations around your property. The bait contains a slow-acting insecticide that termites consume and carry back to their colony, eventually leading to the colony's demise.

Baiting systems offer a continuous monitoring solution and can be less disruptive than liquid treatments in some cases. We will assess your property and infestation to determine if a baiting system is the most appropriate solution.

Wood Treatment

In some instances, direct treatment of infested wood may be necessary. This can involve injecting termiticides directly into termite galleries or applying borate-based products to the wood surface. These treatments target termites actively feeding within the structure and can also make the wood less appealing to future infestations.

Recognizing the Signs: Do You Need Termite Control?

Termites are often called silent destroyers because they can cause significant damage without immediate, obvious signs. However, knowing what to look for can help you detect an infestation in its early stages, potentially saving you from extensive repairs.

Mud Tubes: The Termite Highway

Subterranean termites build mud tubes, which are pencil-sized tunnels made of soil and wood particles, to travel between their underground colonies and their food source (your home). These tubes are usually found on exterior walls, foundation, or in crawl spaces. Finding mud tubes is a strong indication of active termite infestation and requires immediate attention. Breaking open a tube and seeing live termites confirms their presence. These tubes protect the termites from predators and dry air, allowing them to travel safely. They are a clear signal that termites are actively foraging on your property.

Damaged Wood: Hollow Sounds and Bubbling Paint

As termites consume wood from the inside out, the exterior surface may appear undisturbed while the interior is being devoured. Tapping on wood that sounds hollow is a classic sign of termite damage. Additionally, paint that is bubbling or peeling without an obvious moisture source can indicate that termites are active behind the surface. This damage can often be mistaken for water damage, but a closer inspection by a professional can differentiate the cause. Termite-damaged wood can also become soft and easily punctured.

Swarming Termites: A Reproductive Sign

Winged termites, known as swarmers, emerge from mature colonies typically during warm, humid weather to establish new colonies. Seeing swarmers inside or around your home, or finding discarded wings near windowsills and light fixtures, is a sure sign of an active infestation nearby. While swarmers themselves don't cause damage, their presence indicates that a colony is present and reproducing, and that your property is at risk or already infested. These swarms are often mistaken for flying ants, but termites have straight antennae and their two pairs of wings are equal in size.

Termite Droppings (Frass)

Drywood termites expel their fecal pellets, known as frass, near their feeding sites. These pellets are small, often wood-colored, and accumulate in small piles. Finding these piles of frass can indicate a drywood termite infestation. Unlike subterranean termites, drywood termites live entirely within the wood they infest, and their frass is a key identifying characteristic. The shape and color of the frass can even help identify the specific type of drywood termite.

Damaged Drywall or Plaster

Termites can also damage drywall and plaster from behind, leading to sagging or rippling surfaces. This damage is often subtle at first but can worsen over time as the infestation progresses. The damage to drywall can resemble water damage, making proper identification by a professional crucial. If you notice unexplained changes in the appearance of your walls, it could be a sign of hidden termite activity.

Tight-fitting Doors and Windows

As termites consume wood, they can cause structures to become misshapen, leading to doors and windows that are difficult to open or close. This swelling of the wood is a result of the termites' activities within the framing. While this can also be caused by moisture, in conjunction with other signs, it can indicate a termite problem. If your doors and windows suddenly start sticking or are difficult to latch, it's worth investigating for potential termite issues.

Subtle Clicking Sounds

In very quiet environments, you might occasionally hear faint clicking sounds coming from within wood structures. This can be the sound of termites actively chewing and communicating within their galleries. While not a common or easily identifiable sign, in combination with other symptoms, it can contribute to the suspicion of a termite infestation. This sound is typically very subtle and requires a quiet environment to detect.
